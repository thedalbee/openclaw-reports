# Musinsa / Mujinjang AI Ad Contest — Brand Orientation Research

Retrieved: 2026-05-16 23:02–23:08 KST  
Scope: official Musinsa/corporate/newsroom/contest pages, accessible official social metadata, YouTube RSS, and press articles about the contest.

## Key facts

- **Company north star:** Musinsa states its vision as “대한민국을 대표하는 글로벌 최고의 패션 기업 / As Korea’s Dominant Leader, Aspiring To Be The No.1 Global Fashion Company.”
- **Brand/social line:** Official Instagram metadata uses: “우리가 사랑한 패션의 모든 것. 다 무신사랑 해.”
- **Newsroom posture:** Musinsa Newsroom positions itself as official corporate media covering service news, co-growth philosophy, and stories of partner/tenant brands.
- **Contest:** “무진장 성공 기원 AI 광고제” is tied to the 5th anniversary of **무진장 여름 블랙프라이데이**. It asks customers to become ad makers/models using generative AI.
- **Creative latitude:** Official copy explicitly says both “감각적인 영상” and “밈을 활용한 유쾌한 콘텐츠” are welcome; “정답은 없다.”
- **Required assets:** Use Musinsa-provided **무진장 키비주얼 이미지** at least once. Official copy calls out campaign key color **#FE4900**.
- **Format/channel:** 5–30 sec AI video; public SNS upload; required hashtags: `#광고제참여 #무신사 #26여름무진장AI광고제`; then submit through Musinsa/form.
- **Judging/reward:** 27 preliminary selected teams: 5 finalists, 2 special prize, 20 participation prize. Finalists are ranked by Musinsa app/user voting 2026-06-14 to 2026-06-24; final result 2026-06-30. Total prize pool ~KRW 20M; first prize total benefits KRW 10M.
- **Usage:** Finalist/winning work can be used in Musinsa official SNS, third-party channels, online/offline ads, and billboards including Hannam/Gangnam/Megastore Seongsu.

## Strong guardrails

- Must be AI-generated with proof/capture of AI production process.
- Must use commercially usable AI services and disclose tool names.
- Do **not** include recommendation/endorsement or purchase-inducing claims such as “추천합니다,” “꼭 구매하세요,” “제가 사용해보니 좋았습니다.”
- Avoid famous faces/voices/names, identifiable private information, impersonation, defamation, false/exaggerated claims, rights-infringing music/images/characters/voices.
- Winning works may be edited/reformatted by Musinsa, so the concept should tolerate crops, captions, billboard cuts, thumbnails, and SNS reuse.

## What Musinsa seems to promote/reward — evidence, not creative direction

**Facts from official sources:**

- Musinsa frequently emphasizes **curation**, **trend keywords**, **brand experience**, **online/offline integration**, **Seongsu/offline touchpoints**, **benefits/raffles**, and **participating-brand growth** in newsroom campaign copy.
- Recent category festivals (“티셔츠 페스티벌,” “킥스 페스티벌”) combine data/trends, curated themes, brand weeks, live commerce, raffles, offline store benefits, and styling/editorial language.
- Musinsa TV recent RSS feed is mostly short-form “성수기” content with idols/creators, games, colloquial hooks, emojis, and Seongsu/Megastore context.

**Hypotheses:**

- The contest likely tolerates two lanes: **stylish/fashion-sensory AI visual** and **meme/short-form funny execution**, because the official page names both.
- Since final work may run on SNS and billboards, entries probably need a simple visual core that reads quickly and can survive cropping/OOH playback.
- A safer contest angle is “무진장 흥행 기원 / 축제 / abundance / cultural moment” rather than hard-sell “buy this” language.
- Musinsa appears to value customer/creator participation and shareability, not just polished commercial craft.

# General Contest-Winner Reference Summary

Scope: contest-winning works beyond fashion, emphasizing official/primary sources and practical signals for understanding what wins. This is reference material, not creative direction.

## Source coverage

- **AI film/ad contests:** Runway AIFF, Reply AI Film Festival, Curious Refuge x Vica AI Advertising Competition, Project Odyssey, Meta Morph AI Film Award.
- **Short-form/social/video awards:** TikTok Ad Awards DACH/Europe, Shorty Awards, YouTube Works Awards.
- **Advertising award archives/listings:** LIA 2025 official winners/finalists spreadsheet.
- **Blocked or weak sources:** Clio and One Club returned 403; Cannes/LoveTheWork and Webby paths were not usable from this environment.

Detailed structured references are in `references.json`.

## What repeatedly wins

### 1) A one-sentence idea juries can repeat

Winning entries usually have a sharp verbal handle:

- **Total Pixel Space**: every possible digital image / life / perspective.
- **JAILBIRD**: a chicken sent to human prison.
- **DON'T SCREAM**: a horror game where screaming is the mechanic and the marketing.
- **PainVisible**: make invisible pain visible.
- **Daisy vs Scammers**: a named tech character against a clear villain.

Practical signal: if the case needs a long explanation before it feels interesting, it is weaker as contest material.

### 2) Platform-native behavior beats reposted ads

The strongest social/video winners did not simply place a TVC on a platform.

- TikTok Pedro used a meme account, creator collaborations, the sound page, UGC, and Spark Ads.
- CHECK24 turned a free PUMA jersey into a participatory object on TikTok.
- Nissan's YouTube Works Grand Prix used a 4-hour LoFi format for an EV, matching product attribute to YouTube behavior.
- BFG Retail CU won for Shorts as fandom-building, not just reach.

Practical signal: judges reward work that understands the native grammar of the channel.

### 3) Participation mechanics matter

Many winners gave the audience something to do, wear, generate, remix, or perform.

- Spider-Verse: 1.5M AI Spidersonas, UGC trailer recreation, fan programs.
- CHECK24: users wore jerseys, made videos, joined prediction-game culture.
- Pedro: users adopted the sound and meme language.
- DON'T SCREAM: streamers' reactions became the distribution engine.

Practical signal: participation is strongest when it is structurally tied to the product/campaign, not bolted on.

### 4) Product truth becomes format truth

Winners often make the product's actual property determine the creative form.

- Nissan Ariya EV quietness -> peaceful LoFi ambient video.
- DON'T SCREAM microphone mechanic -> viral scream/reaction content.
- Fleurop Mother's Day urgency -> timed full-funnel creator + delivery campaign.
- O2 anti-scam purpose -> functional tech character/scambait system.

Practical signal: the best cases can answer, “Why could only this brand/product do this?”

### 5) Culture is used as infrastructure, not decoration

- STōK used Wrexham AFC and stadium naming rights; the odd fact of sponsoring in a cold-brew-light market became the hook.
- Barbie used a single cultural code — pink, nostalgia, play — across many touchpoints.
- Scream VI translated Ghostface's character behavior across Ring, TikTok Live, red carpet stunts, and NYC activations.
- TikTok winners tied into existing music, football, Mother's Day, and UEFA moments.

Practical signal: culture wins when it creates distribution and participation, not just references.

### 6) Multi-category winners are usually behavior-changing ideas

LIA examples show repeated Golds when an idea stretches across PR, social, direct, health, integration, and content categories:

- **Vaseline Verified**: Gold across social, PR, commerce, strategy, health, integration.
- **The Final Copy of Ilon Specht**: Gold across brand IP, documentary, online film, TV/cinema, PR, strategy.
- **AXA Three Words**, **L'Oréal**, **O2 Daisy vs Scammers**, etc. show the same pattern.

Practical signal: multi-category strength comes from a core idea that changes public behavior, reframes a brand asset, or creates a usable system.

### 7) AI winners are not just “AI-looking”

AI-specific contests still reward storytelling, clarity, and craft:

- Runway AIFF: Grand Prix and Gold are described through premise and perspective, not software.
- Reply AIFF required both the film and an explanation of AI use.
- Curious Refuge's AI ad contest required AI in every shot, but also real brand briefs and a 30-second commercial constraint.
- PainVisible won in an AI-enabled medical category because AI clarifies a real human/medical problem.

Practical signal: AI must either unlock the premise, improve the production logic, or create utility. Visual novelty alone is fragile.

## Useful contest-evaluation checklist

When scanning or preparing contest-style ad references, score each candidate on these axes:

1. **Single-line hook:** Can the work be summarized in one memorable sentence?
2. **Brand/product necessity:** Is the idea specific to the brand/product, or could anyone do it?
3. **Native format:** Does it use the channel's actual behavior, not just its inventory?
4. **Participation:** Does the audience have a natural action to take?
5. **Proof:** Are there metrics beyond views — sales, ROAS, UGC count, streams, followers, search rank, wishlists?
6. **Cultural timing:** Does it ride an existing moment with a clear reason?
7. **Craft fit:** Does the craft style serve the idea rather than showing off?
8. **Category elasticity:** Could the idea plausibly win in more than one category?
9. **Case readability:** Would a judge understand the problem, solution, and result in under 60 seconds?
